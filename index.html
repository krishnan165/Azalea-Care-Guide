Azalea Care Guide

Soil for Azalea

Well-draining, acidic soil with organic matter.

Azaleas thrive in soil that is rich in organic content and maintains acidity.

Watering

Consistent watering, especially during dry periods.

Keep the soil evenly moist to support Azaleas' hydration needs.

Pruning

Prune after flowering to shape and control size.

Pruning helps maintain a compact and attractive Azalea plant.

Colors

Benefits

Beautiful and vibrant blooms in various colors.

Azaleas are known for their stunning floral displays during the blooming season.

Adaptable to different garden styles.

They can enhance both formal and informal garden designs.

Non-Benefits

May be sensitive to alkaline soil; avoid excessive lime application.
